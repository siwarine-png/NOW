# 168 Hours
## You Have More Time Than You Think
**Author:** Laura Vanderkam

---

# Core Idea

Everyone has the same **168 hours every week**.

The problem is usually **not a lack of time**, but **how those hours are allocated**.

The goal is not to become more efficient at everything.

The goal is to intentionally spend more hours on what creates the greatest value.

---

# Key Principles

## 1. Think in Weeks

Don't optimize one day.

Optimize the entire week.

Instead of asking:

> "How do I fit this into today?"

Ask:

> "Where does this belong in my 168 hours?"

---

## 2. Measure Reality First

Most people underestimate how much time they lose.

Track one complete week.

Example:

```text
07:00 Wake

07:30 Breakfast

08:15 Social Media

09:00 Work

10:30 Email

11:00 Meeting

12:00 Lunch

...
```

Reality is often different from memory.

---

## 3. Build Around Priorities

Fill your calendar with important things first.

Typical priorities:

- Health
- Family
- Career
- Learning
- Business
- Recovery

Everything else fits around them.

---

## 4. Eliminate Time Leaks

Ask:

- Must I do this?
- Can it be automated?
- Can someone else do it?
- Can I stop doing it?

Examples:

- unnecessary meetings
- excessive email
- endless scrolling
- repetitive admin work

---

## 5. Batch Similar Work

Avoid context switching.

Instead of:

```text
Email

Coding

Meeting

Email

Design

Coding
```

Use:

```text
09:00–11:00 Deep Work

11:00–11:30 Email

13:00–15:00 Meetings

15:00–17:00 Design
```

---

## 6. Design an Ideal Week

Create recurring blocks.

Example:

Monday
- Planning
- Deep Work

Tuesday
- Product Development

Wednesday
- Meetings

Thursday
- Research

Friday
- Review

Weekend
- Recovery
- Family
- Learning

---

## 7. Invest in High-Value Work

Spend more hours where only you create value.

Examples:

High Value

- Building products
- Writing
- Research
- Strategy
- Learning
- Creating systems

Lower Value

- Inbox
- Busywork
- Administrative tasks
- Repetitive chores

---

## 8. Plan Recovery

Rest is part of productivity.

Schedule:

- sleep
- exercise
- reading
- hobbies
- vacations
- family

Recovery is an investment.

---

# Weekly Review

At the end of each week ask:

## Planned

How did I intend to spend my 168 hours?

## Actual

Where did my hours actually go?

## Gap

What caused the difference?

## Improve

What should change next week?

# Action Checklist

Every Sunday:

- Review last week's hours.
- Identify time leaks.
- Choose three highest priorities.
- Block time for them first.
- Schedule everything else afterward.
- Protect those blocks.
- Review at week's end.

Repeat every week.

---

# Lessons

- Think in weeks, not days.
- Measure reality before making changes.
- Time is allocated, not found.
- Eliminate low-value work.
- Protect your most important hours.
- Recovery is productive.
- Consistency beats perfection.
